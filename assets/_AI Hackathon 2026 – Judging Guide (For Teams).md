# **AI Hackathon – Judging Guide (For Teams)**

## **How Your Solution Will Be Evaluated**

To ensure transparency and consistency, all solutions will be evaluated across the following criteria in **two stages**:

* ### Technical Review (Pre-Review)

Focus: **Depth, rigor, feasibility, responsible AI**

* ### Demo Day Evaluation

Focus: **Functionality, clarity, usability, impact**

## **Judging Criteria**

| Criteria | Weight | What It Means | What Judges Look For |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Mission Relevance | 🔴 High | Solves a real government problem | Clear alignment to use case, real-world applicability |
| Technical Soundness | 🔴 High | Strong, working AI solution | End-to-end functionality, solid architecture |
| Explainability & Responsible AI | 🔴 High | Transparent and trustworthy AI | Explainable outputs, human-in-the-loop, bias awareness |
| Feasibility for Adoption | 🔴 High | Can this be used in government? | Practical design, realistic path to deployment |
| Innovation | 🟡 Medium | Creative use of AI | Novel approach, forward-thinking solution |
| Demo Clarity | 🟡 Medium | Clear and compelling presentation | Easy to understand, well-structured demo |

### 

### **1\. Mission Relevance (High Weight)**

#### **What it means**

Your solution should clearly address the **core government problem** defined in the use case.

#### **What judges look for**

Technical Review:

* Clear alignment to the **mission need**  
* Understanding of **real-world constraints**  
* Relevance of the AI approach to the problem

Demo Day:

* Is the problem clearly explained?  
* Does the solution **actually solve it**?  
* Is the **value to the agency obvious**?

### **2\. Technical Soundness (High Weight)**

#### **What it means**

Your solution should be **technically solid, well-designed, and functional**.

#### **What judges look for**

**Technical Review:**

* Appropriate use of AI/ML techniques  
* Data pipeline clarity (ingestion → processing → output)  
* Architecture and design decisions  
* Working prototype (not just slides)

**Demo Day:**

* Does the solution **work end-to-end**?  
* Is the system stable and coherent?  
* Are key features demonstrated effectively?

### **3\. Explainability & Responsible AI (High Weight)**

#### **What it means**

Your solution must provide **transparent, understandable outputs** and demonstrate **responsible use of AI**.

#### **What judges look for**

**Technical Review:**

* Explainable outputs (not “black box”)  
* Traceability of decisions  
* Consideration of bias, fairness, or risk  
* Human-in-the-loop where appropriate

**Demo Day:**

* Can you clearly explain **how the AI reached its conclusions**?  
* Are outputs understandable to a **non-technical audience**?  
* Is there evidence of **safe and responsible design**?

### **4\. Feasibility for Adoption (High Weight)**

#### **What it means**

Your solution should be **realistically deployable** in a government environment.

#### **What judges look for**

**Technical Review:**

* Practical architecture  
* Use of **available data sources**  
* Reasonable integration approach  
* Awareness of constraints (security, scale, governance)

**Demo Day:**

* Could an agency **actually use this**?  
* Is there a clear path from MVP → production?  
* Is the solution **practical, not theoretical**?

### **5\. Innovation (Medium Weight)**

#### **What it means**

Your solution should demonstrate **creative or novel use of AI**.

#### **What judges look for**

**Technical Review:**

* Unique approach to solving the problem  
* Creative use of models, data, or workflows

**Demo Day:**

* Does the solution feel **different or forward-thinking**?  
* Does it go beyond a basic implementation?

### **6\. Demo Clarity (Medium Weight)**

#### **What it means**

Your team should clearly communicate **what you built, how it works, and why it matters**.

#### **What judges look for**

**Technical Review:**

* Clear documentation or explanation of approach

**Demo Day:**

* Clear, concise storytelling  
* Logical demo flow  
* Easy-to-understand explanation of value  
* Effective use of time

## **What This Means for Teams**

To succeed in this hackathon, your solution should:

* Solve a **real mission problem**  
* Demonstrate **working AI capabilities**  
* Provide **clear, explainable outputs**  
* Be **practical for government use**  
* Be presented in a **clear and compelling way**
